State of Linux Gaming in 2021

Introduction

Gaming in Linux has been a really interesting topic on the Linux circle lately due to the immense progress that has been made for the past couple of years.

A decade ago, there where only a handful of native Linux triple A games such as Amnesia, Valve games such as Half Life and portal and hundreds of indie native Linux games such as Frogatto, Tux games (SuperTux and Tuxcart) and Wine was still at its infancy but very promising (so much so that it can even run Starcraft 2!)

Now jump back to present, alot of things happened. OpenGL was always a bit inferior compare to DirectX in terms of performance. Then AMD invested in FOSS laying their Mantle API to the FOSS community and Vulkan was born. Not to mention AMD opensourced their drivers too.

Vulkan made alot of things possible. Such as DXVK and VKD3D. Originally Wine had great compatibility with DirectX9 and lower games. However since newer games where using DirectX10, DirectX11 and DirectX12. It was thought to be difficult for Wine to catch up with the newer iterations of DirectX. With DXVK, DirectX10 and DirectX11 games became a reality and VKD3D became the solution for the new DirectX12.

In addition Vulkan allowed more room for native Linux game because it was cross-platform specification in constrast to DirectX which was Windows only.

We already knew for quite awhile now that Valve went all in on Linux. This started first with their games but they even created Proton which is a Wine compatibility layer for Steam to allow to play Windows games on Linux! Because of this move, Valve opened alot of games to Linux users. (Case in point; I was able to even play Resident Evil 3 and Trials of Mana on Day 1!)

Because of Proton as well, we started to have a flood of Wine forks aiming to be compatible to a specific software/game which is always a great news to Linux fans.

Now with such a very long introduction, the question now is. How good is Linux gaming? Are we there yet? In the next section I will discuss what we have, the challenges we have and what we may never get.

State of DXVK

Despite Microsoft pushing everyone to DirectX12 and DirectX12 becoming the norm for newer games. Alot of developers are still using DirectX11. DXVK is still at its infancy stages. For sure there are alot of games already DXVK supports such as the ones I mentioned earlier, Resident Evil 3, Trials of Mana, in addition, Control and Death Stranding. Should be mentioned that these games are not even a year old yet!
